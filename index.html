REF — AI READINESS DIAGNOSTIC

How ready is this environment for AI?

A structured read on stakeholder, process, data, and governance readiness — plus a separate ecosystem score — scored against the scale of the initiative you're actually planning.

Stakeholder 01

The people who will use the AI initiative day-to-day were involved in defining what it should do.

Leadership has a written definition of what success looks like for the AI initiative.

Leadership has agreed in writing on when success measurement for the AI initiative begins.

At least one person on the team has hands-on experience with a comparable AI tool.

A single stakeholder has authority to approve the AI initiative's next steps without committee sign-off.

Process 02

The AI initiative's core workflows are documented.

No in-person knowledge transfer is required for someone new to follow the AI initiative's core workflows.

The AI initiative's core workflows have no duplicate manual data entry between systems.

The AI initiative's core workflows have a named owner.

The AI initiative's core workflows are reviewed on a set schedule.

Data 03

The data critical to the AI initiative lives in accessible, documented systems.

A named owner is accountable for data quality in the area the AI initiative would touch.

The data supporting the AI initiative covers at least 12 months of history.

The data supporting the AI initiative is in structured format (database or spreadsheet), not free text or scanned documents.

The data supporting the AI initiative integrates with core enterprise systems of record, not a standalone extract.

Governance 04

A named executive is accountable for decisions made in the AI initiative.

A named executive is accountable for the results of the AI initiative.

There's a defined process for reviewing the AI initiative's outputs for risk, compliance, or bias before they reach real decisions.

There's a way to monitor and course-correct the AI system after launch.

The AI initiative's scope is small enough to not require a formal compliance review before starting.

There is a company-wide AI governance policy that the AI initiative falls under.

Ecosystem 05 — Scored separately

AI tools or copilots are used in daily workflows by real teams — not a one-time pilot that quietly stopped.

More than one function or department already uses some form of AI — it isn't confined to a single isolated team.

There's an established vendor or platform stack for AI tools, rather than scattered one-off experiments per team.

Handoffs between AI output and human review are clearly defined — people know exactly when to trust it and when to check it.

Workflows were actually redesigned around AI, rather than AI bolted onto the same steps that existed before it.
